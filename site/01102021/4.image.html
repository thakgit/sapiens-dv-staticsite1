Namak Para - The perfect snack for your evening tea

Move aside pretzels. We have our own desi version that tastes way better. Follow our recipe to make the crunchiest, yummiest namak paras. Find a window seat, make a hot cup of tea, and take a handful of these namak paras to elevate the snacking experience. Make it for Diwali and Holi, add it to your chaats, or just munch away. These will find a way to your plate however you want.

Ingredients:

  • Maida/all purpose flour – 2 cups
  • Oil – 4 tbsp
  • Ajwain(onion seeds) – 1 tsp
  • Dried Kasuri methi – 1 tsp
  • Kalonji (caraway seeds) – 1 tsp
  • Water – 1/2 cup
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Steps:

  1. Take maida in a bowl and add oil to it.
  2. Rub maida and oil together until they start binding together. To check if your maida is mixed well, pick a handful of maida and make a fist. If the maida forma a ball/stays together and does not crumble, then you have enough oil for your namak para in the maida to make it crunchy.
  3. Now add salt, crushed kasuri methi, ajwain (caraway seeds) and kalonji (onion seeds) and mix well.
  4. Add water gradually and form a smooth, tight dough. It is important that you knead the dough well for about 4-5 mins.
  5. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15 mins.
  6. After 15 mins, take a small ball of the dough and roll it like chapatis. The thickness should be appx. double that of chapati.
  7. Cut the rolled dough into strips horizontally and then cut across the strips to get small rectangles of namak paras.
  8. Heat oil on medium heat, drop a small batch of namak paras, and fry until they turn golden. This takes around 5-6 mins.
  9. Let them cool down a little. They might feel a little soft right off the oil but will turn crunchy once they cool down.
  10. Enjoy the namak paras with tea, coffee, or as is. You can also use them in place of papri in papri chaat.

Cheese Spread - simple and delicious spread for crackers , chips and sandwiches

Glam up your next party with this yummy cheese spread that is simple and easy to make. Itâ€™s so addictive that you will find yourself using it on everything you eat!!!

Ingredients:

  • Cream cheese – 1 cup ( room temperature)
  • Hung curd / plain Greek yoghurt – 1/4th cup
  • Heavy cream – 2 tbsp
  • Grated carrots – 2 tbsp
  • Chopped onions – 2 tbsp
  • Coriander leaves – a few
  • Chaat masala – 1 tsp
  • Roasted cumin pwd – 1 tsp
  • Salt – a pinch

Steps:

  1. Take a mixing bowl and add cream cheese to it. Weight watchers can use reduced-fat cream cheese.
  2. Add hung curd. You can use plain greek yogurt instead.
  3. Add heavy cream. (don’t skip to add cream as it balances the taste and gives a rich flavor and nice spreadable consistency).
  4. Add grated carrots, finely chopped onion, and chopped coriander (cilantro) leaves.
  5. Time to add chaat masala, roasted cumin powder, and a pinch of salt (here we are using salted cream cheese so, we are using less salt)
  6. give it a good mix and it’s ready!!
  7. Enjoy it with crackers, chips, and a sandwich spread.
